## 1. Source Hierarchy

Use sources in this order whenever possible:

1. official transport, attraction, hotel, venue, local government, or official chain-hotel pages
2. large public travel platforms, map listings, and mainstream public guides
3. user-generated content and review platforms for sentiment and pitfall signals
4. Xiaohongshu for vibe, complaint patterns, and worth-it judgment

Use higher-tier sources for factual claims. Use lower-tier sources mainly for experience, vibe, and repeated complaints.

## 2. Lodging Source Preference

For hotels, prefer this lookup order:

1. official chain booking ecosystems or official brand pages
2. chain hotel listings from large public travel platforms
3. non-chain hotels from large public travel platforms
4. homestays or other alternative lodging only when relevant

Mainstream chain brands worth checking first include Huazhu, Atour, and other common midscale chains that fit the user's budget, convenience needs, and comfort expectations.

If no suitable chain options are found, say so explicitly before widening the search.

## 3. Citation Policy

Attach sources for any key fact that could affect the user's decision.

Key facts include:

- transport price ranges
- train or flight timing
- station or airport access facts
- hotel ratings and review context
- attraction opening hours
- ticket prices
- reservation or entry restrictions
- location convenience claims

Preferred pattern:

- put the claim first
- attach one or more links immediately after the claim
- if the fact may have changed, add `待确认`

## 4. Confidence Labels

Use these labels consistently:

- `已确认`: supported by recent authoritative or clearly matching sources
- `较可信`: supported by multiple public sources but still time-sensitive
- `待确认`: missing, conflicting, stale, or weakly sourced

Do not mark dynamic pricing or fast-changing schedules as `已确认` unless the source is clearly current.

## 5. Xiaohongshu Guidance

Treat Xiaohongshu as an auxiliary sentiment layer and an active search surface when accessible.

Use it for:

- whether a spot still feels worth going
- common regret points
- whether a neighborhood matches a certain trip style
- photo-only versus experience-rich signals
- repeated complaints about queues, value, service, or overhype

Do not use it as the sole source for:

- opening hours
- ticket prices
- transit instructions
- hotel factual details

When reading Xiaohongshu-style evidence:

- prefer several independent creators over one hot post
- discount obvious sponsorship or templated promotion
- watch for repeated keywords such as `人挤`, `排队`, `出片`, `鸡肋`, `不值`, `方便`, `踩雷`
- when sentiment is split, downgrade to `待判断`

## 6. Hard Failure Cases

Consider the output incomplete if any of these happen:

- transport or hotel recommendation lacks source links for key facts
- area recommendation is made without area-level reasoning
- no chain hotel check is mentioned before jumping to random properties
- a place is added to the main plan despite repeated severe negative signals
- stale or uncertain facts are written as certain
